Frequently Asked Questions (Medical)

What is the difference between Class 1 and Class 2 medical?

Class 2 is the initial medical required for Student Pilot License (SPL) or PPL, while Class 1 is mandatory before applying for Commercial Pilot License (CPL).

Who conducts the Class 2 medical?

Class 2 medicals are conducted by DGCA-approved doctors (AMEs) across India. You can locate them in the AME Locator section.

How do I apply for a Class 1 medical?

After clearing Class 2, you must apply via eGCA and book an appointment at an approved center like AFCME, IAM (Bangalore), or Apollo (Delhi).

Do I need perfect eyesight for Class 1 medical?

You must have 6/6 vision, but corrective lenses (spectacles) are allowed. Color blindness is not permitted for pilots.

How long are the medicals valid?

Class 2 is valid for 2 years if you're under 40. Class 1 is valid for 1 year, and needs renewal depending on age and license type.
